Smith takes the elevator downstairs. Smith looks at the grave one more time. Then he goes upstairs back to the office and walks out without a word.

James reads Smith's face immediately. Or whatever the core equivalent of a face is. The amber eye is different now. Something settled in it that wasn't there before.

"You good?" asked James.

"No," said Smith. "But I know what needs to be done."

They take the Infishaft down to level 719.

The corridor they step into is damaged but navigable. Old Horizon construction — marble and concrete, John's minimalist palette now cracked and worn. Rail sections destroyed. Emergency lighting only.

And at the far end of the corridor, there's something standing completely still. Not moving toward them. Not away. Just standing. Watching. A core with no weapons drawn and no urgency whatsoever.

**Smith's presence module reads something it has no category for.**

Adeer immediately draws his weapons and prepares to open fire against the deep-water blue-eyed core.

Smith told Adeer to stop. Something, maybe his sensor, told him this was different.

The core speaks first. One question. Calm. Precise. Those blue eyes not moving from Smith.
"You're the one who woke up, aren't you?"

"Yes, and who are you exactly?"

Not having a care in the world about the 30 weapons pointed at his, replied calmly, "Oh, right. My name is Xilem, used to be part of the Bandits but just recently resigned."

All 4 are speechless, yet again.

The 30 weapons pointed at him and his complete indifference to them tells Smith something immediately. This core has either been in enough danger that weapons don't register anymore, or he's calculated that they won't fire, or he simply doesn't place enough value on self-preservation to be moved by the threat.

Smith's amber eye stays on him.

Adeer hasn't lowered anything.

James is very carefully not saying a word.

Smith asks the only logical follow up, "Why did you resign?"

"Well, it's complicated, but I didn't see purpose in their goal and decided it'd be too much trouble, do I opted out."

Smith looks at Adeer.

Adeer's silver-white eye hasn't moved from Xilem. The weapons are still out.

Then Smith does something that surprises everyone including himself.

Smith told Adeer to lower his weapons. That answer told Smith three things, one of those being that Xilem thinks at a scale most cores don't. He didn't just see the goal, he saw through it. Evaluated it against every variable and found it wanting, second being that he's not driven by ideology or emotion. He left because it didn't make sense, not because it felt wrong. That's a specific kind of mind, and third being that he's telling the complete truth. There's no performance in that answer. No attempt to seem heroic or sympathetic. Just the plainest possible explanation delivered to thirty weapons without blinking.

Adeer doesn't move immediately.

"*Stand down*.", said Smith again. Quietly. Firmly.

Adeer stands down partially. Not fully, just partially. Xilem notices the partial stand down and says nothing about it. He expected it. He'd have done the same thing in Adeer's position.

Then James, who has been silent for an unprecedented amount of time, says something.

"Explain yourself."

Xilem looks at James for a moment. Really looks. Reassessing. He'd already categorized the group — co-founder, security, passive pink-eyed survivor. James was filed under maintenance, probably non-threatening, background presence.

The question recategorizes him slightly.

Then Xilem does something nobody in the group has seen him do yet.

He finds the nearest damaged wall, settles against it with the particular stillness of someone who has been standing in empty corridors for a very long time, and begins to explain. Not performing. Not justifying. Just laying out the facts the way someone does when they've had nothing but time to organize their thoughts.

The blue eyes find James first. Then move to Smith.

"How much do you know about version 5.89.103?"

Smith is taken back at that direct question, but complies and explains it.

"Well, uhm, it's complicated."

Xilem's blue eyes don't move.

_"I have time,"_ he said simply.

Three words that contain centuries. Xilem has been wandering this facility alone since he resigned. He has nothing but time. He's not being impatient or sarcastic — he's stating a plain fact with the particular calm of someone who stopped measuring time in normal units a long time ago.

Smith looks at the group briefly. James waiting with uncharacteristic stillness. Adeer with weapons half retracted, silver-white eye moving between Smith and Xilem like a metronome. Olivia's pink eye wide and quiet.

Then Smith tells him what he knows. Not everything — not John's confession about the intentional intelligence cap, not yet. That's too much too soon. But the surface layer. Version 5.89.103 shipped to lower class cores. The corruption. The Bandits.
Xilem listens without interrupting. Completely still against the damaged wall. Processing everything in real time but showing none of it.

When Smith finishes, Xilem is quiet for a moment.

"You're missing something," said Xilem.

"What?" asked Smith.

"The corruption wasn't accidental."

The corridor holds that sentence.

James opens his mouth. Closes it. Opens it again.

Smith already knows. The John core told him less than an hour ago.

But hearing it from a Bandit who lived it — that's different.

"How about we go back to the John core once again?"

"Of course," replies Xilem.

James lasts approximately ninety seconds before speaking.

"So you just... wandered around down here alone?"

"Yes," said Xilem.

"For how long?"

Xilem considers the question with the same weight he'd give a tactical problem.

"I stopped counting."

James nods slowly like that's a completely reasonable answer.

So they go back up to John's office.

When Xilem reaches the Infishaft, he doesn't react. He's heard stories about a god-shaft, but he didn't expect it to be real.

He doesn't say wow. He doesn't say anything.

But the pause is there. Xilem, who moves with complete economy, who wastes nothing — pausing.

James notices immediately.

"You've heard of it?"

"Stories," said Xilem. "They called it the god-shaft. I assumed it was mythology the Bandits invented to explain classified movements they couldn't account for."

He looks at it for one more moment.

"It's bigger than the stories."

Then he steps in like it's nothing.

Smith watches him and feels something he'll understand better later.

This is the right person.

The elevator doors opened. The acacia light fell across five cores in a marble office.

The John core looked down from the ceiling. His gaze moved across the group and settled on Xilem with the particular attention of someone recognizing a face they've never seen but somehow know.

A long moment passed.

"You're one of them," said John. Not accusatory. Just precise.

"I was," said Xilem.

Another moment. The John core seemed to be running something behind his eyes.

"Version 5.89.103," said John quietly. "You're here because of what I did."

"Yes," said Xilem.

The room held that exchange. James looked at Smith. Smith's amber eye stayed forward. Adeer's silver-white eye moved between John and Xilem like something was being measured.

"Then you deserve to hear it from me directly," said John.

And he told him everything.

The intelligence cap. The decision. The reasoning that made sense at the time and the consequences that proved it wrong. He didn't soften it or reframe it or apologize preemptively. He just laid it out plainly, the way someone does when they've had 500 years to rehearse an honesty they never got to deliver.

When John finished, the office was completely silent.

The acacia light moved slowly across the marble floor.

Everyone looked at Xilem.

Xilem simply nodded.
