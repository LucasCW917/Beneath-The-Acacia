*Drip*. Smith woke to water on his camera lens and nothing else.

"Why am I here?"

Smith had awoken after an unknown period of pure silence in the Horizon facility. Now, Smith wanted to check out what happened.

After some wandering along his tracks, he reached a door. To Smith this was normal. After all, he was the one who helped the founder, *John Horizon*, found and build the *900*-floor deep facility.

After a quick eye scan, the door let his through. It was as if it was yesterday the facility was still pristine, and a mere 24-hours later it felt *deserted*, *abandoned*. A sign above read "No pain, no gain", ironic since his pain as in loneliness didn't return any gain.

Wandering aimlessly, his memory began to resurface. He knew the facility by heart when he used to work here, just he couldn't recall those memories. 

Smith wandered for so long he lost count of time, which was strange since as a Horizon core he was equipped with the best of time trackers. 

Then, he spotted it, a rusted core buried in the dirt of a deserted corridor. Smith wanted not to care, but the loneliness was getting to him. "Hey!" No response. "Hey! Sir, are you there?" Still, no response. Conveniently, there was a core attachment station nearby, so Smith navigated there and grabbed the arm attachment to pick the core up and mount him back to the track.

*Clang*. A low, electrical humming ensued for about 5 seconds - long enough for Smith to wonder if it still worked.. Then, to Smiths utter surprise, the core booted up and the eye-camera lit up a greenish-blue. This core sounded deeper, like a middle-aged man living in the suburbs.

"The hell?", said the core. "What is this pl- and who are **you**?", asked the core. Smith answered that he himself also had no clue and he was just wandering when he saw this core and decided to boot him up. "Well, the names James. Nice to meet you, what are you called?", asked the core. Smith answered swiftly and they both stared at each other, unable to comprehend what was happening.

"Do you know where we are?", asked Smith. "Well, do I look like Einstein to you?" "Hey, I was just asking, you know."

Wandering together, they really started to enjoy the scenery together. Green bushes, nature taking over. It was also quite unsettling as the facility didn't seem to have any power, made obvious by the red emergency lighting humming from above them.

Then they saw the elevator shaft. It read level 648, and inside the shaft indicated they were about 3,240 meters deep. Out of curiosity, they pressed the call elevator button and waited. To their amazement, an elevator arrived, seemingly powered by a different power source, indicated by the power source sign that indicated it was powered by a battery.

"Damn, batteries power this thing?", exclaimed James, "Well, where do we go?"

"I don't know. What does the information panel say?", asked Smith.

"Well, it says this thing services from floors 800-300, with the exception of level 330.", said James. "Well, where do you want to go?" "Well, I'm going to press a floor by random, okay?"

*Click*. *Beep.* They heard what can only be described as horrifying motor sounds and the sound of brakes disengaging and re-engaging. Then, the voice of a woman said, "Sector F, going to (*delay*) level 356. Please ensure all limbs are inside the elevator cabin. Doors are (*delay*) closing."

5 minutes of pure terror. In that window, they heard several beams snap, glass shattering, the intercom saying: "We apologize for the slight technical difficulties we are facing. Again, please (*voice crack*) ensure all limbs are (*delay*) in the elevator cabin.", brakes falling off and a cable snapping.

*Mechanical grinding*. *Brakes engaging*. *Cables locking into place*. It sounded like a resurrection ritual gone sideways. "You have arrived at (*delay*) level three hundred (*corrupted sounding*) fifty six. Would you like to (*delay*) tip the elevator?", said the elevator. "Tips? Really?", muttered James. 

James and Smith both look up from the elevator screen. Their jaw **drops**. Another core, fully powered on, is looking them up and down. "Now, sir, care to tell me who you are?", said James and Smith simultaneously. "Well, I heard the elevator, figured someone was coming, whether it be ally or enemy. Also, the names Adeer."

"I reckon I've been here for a few months, give or take. I'm not sure how I got here, but I know somethings seriously wrong here."

"How come?", asked Smith.

"I'm pretty sure just a few floors below is the place we were all born - the **supercomputer**.", said Adeer.

"A supercomputer, you say?", said Smith, "That sounds - oddly familiar, yet I can't seem to put my tongue on it."

"Oh hey, look, a computer!", exclaimed James. All three turned to the left where a computer kiosk was installed into the wall.

They all agreed to turn it on.

*Click*. *Jet engine sounds*. Bold text appeared on the screen, and it read: "**HORIZON SYSTEMS**". Below was a username box and a password box. All three don't have any memory of their employee credentials. Luckily, there was a public credentials directory in the system, so they decided to look themselves up.

Everybody stares at the screen. "Holy fuck", says James a moment later. Nobody disagrees with him. They're been inactive for so long they forgot who they were entirely. Nobody seemed to realize the power that Smith had.

Smith was the first to log into his workspace through his eye scan. Inside was a map of the lower levels, a blueprint of himself, the rail network status which was blood red, indicating the desperate need for a replacement, a personnel registry, and a folder named **J.H.**. Smith knows what it is. It's been there since the workspace was created. It's the only thing on the workspace that isn't purely functional.

"What's inside the J.H. folder?", asked James and Adeer. "I don't really know, well, let's click in and see.", said Smith.

Smith feels something that he can't quite explain yet.

Inside the folder was pictures of Smith as a human, with another person neither knew. Then there were pictures of the core they were logged into with the same unknown person.

Smith feels a sense of warmth. He knows who that is. That was his best friend from when he was human. "Why do I know this?", asked Smith to himself. He decided he didn't want to know.

They logged off and continued to explore.
