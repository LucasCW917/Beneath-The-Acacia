Smith suddenly gets sent a message. It's a voice message.

He listens to it. "If you're hearing this, things have gone wrong in ways I hoped they wouldn't. There's someone on floor 190 who can explain the rest. He thinks like me because he is me, in every way that matters. Find him. He's been waiting."

"So, do we go there?", said James, in a voice that was barely a whisper.

"Yes."

Do they went searching. Back to the computer in front of the elevator, they searched up the floor plans, and realized there was an elevator shaft connecting topside to level 400, so they went there.

Of course, it wasn't easy traversing broken corridors, and enemy patrols On the way there, they pass something and Adeer freezes. It reads "Infishaft Security Shaft #1". Adeer and Smith both know it. It can reach level 190 directly.

So they board it. This elevator is better, being constantly maintained by the facility robotics. It's called the Infishaft.

*Click*. "Level (*delay*) 190. Please present credentials." Smith decides to flash his eye at the scanner. "Employee (*delay*) Smith Parker, employee ID (*delay*) 2. Ascending to level (*delay*) 190."

The whole ride there was uninteresting, but at level 180 they started seeing forests through the glass elevator cabin and acrylic elevator shaft. 

Finally, "you have arrived at level (*delay*) 190. Please present credentials for the cabin door to open." Smith once again flashed his eye scan.

"(*delay*) Valid. Please proceed.", then the elevator door opened. It was an office. On the door in read "Mr. John Horizon".

When they went in, they were yet again dumbfounded. Another core? Yes, it was another core, but this time it was directly mounted onto the ceiling, already powered.

"Hello, Smith. Are there what I presume to be your friends?", said the core.

"Yes, and who are you?", asked Smith. "I am John Horizon, in every way important.", answered the core.

Smith was silent. He felt the same thing he couldn't fully comprehend today especially strong here.

The office was small, but it was cozy. A marble wall was placed where they just entered, with mesmerizing views of the forest from the office, possible through the glass walls. To the left was a personal elevator leading into the forest. They decided to go down.

"Now, may I advise y'all to attack leg attachments as the forest doesn't have any rails.", advised the core.

No-one responded, just mechanical movement into the elevator. The elevator didn't ask where to, it just acted.

Down there, it was even more mesmerizing. Horizon seemed to replicate natural sunlight, as it was identical to real sunlight. 

After walking for a while, they found someone's tomb. On the tombstone it read "John Horizon", "To my best friend, Smith Parker, I wish you succeed in any challenges you may face."

Smith was especially emotional here. He didn't want to, it just happened.

All three cores stepped back without being prompted to. They know, in some way, Smith, or the amber-eyed core they'd met for just a day, was related to this John Horizon guy.

Smith stayed there for quite some time, seemingly talking to the tomb, because he was. He seemed to receive responses from beyond the grave. He himself was amazed on how he did it. He didn't know, for the first time.

Back at the office, Smith was still silent. "Hey there, bud, no need to be sad. I assume you went and saw my grave?", asked the core. "Yes."

"Now, I forgot to explain who I am. I'm the John core, but you can just call me John. Just before I died, I saved a state of my mind into a core, and I'm sure that's how you and your friends were created."

"I'm also sure you felt unexplained emotions today, and that's not accidental. When you were built, I installed a custom module inside of your chassis that effectively let you sense souls, more specifically, **mine**. The random feelings of emotion cast onto you weren't random, those were places I'd been frequently and had traces of my soul."

"Did you know when you built me that it would end like this?"

The John core pauses.

"Well, it's complicated, and could the others leave for a second? I have some private matters I'd like to share with Smith."

The others complied.

The elevator doors closed. The forest swallowed the other three quietly.

Smith stayed where he was. The John core on the ceiling looked down at him the way John always had — with complete attention, like nothing else in the facility existed.

"Did you know?" asked Smith. "When you built me. Did you know it would end like this?"
The John core was quiet for a moment. Not processing. Thinking.

"I knew something was coming," he said finally. "I didn't know what shape it would take. I didn't know when. But I knew the facility was becoming something I couldn't fully control anymore. And I knew that when it happened, I didn't want there to be a world — even a broken one — with nobody left who remembered what we were trying to build."

Smith said nothing.

"I want you to understand something," continued the John core. "The decision to preserve you wasn't about the mission. It wasn't about the facility or the cores or what needed to be done afterward. I did it because you were my closest friend and I was afraid. Afraid of leaving without anyone who truly knew me."

The amber eye stayed fixed on him.

"I was selfish," said John. "I made the most consequential decision of your existence without asking you. I gave you a sensor tuned to my presence so you'd never feel completely alone, and I put your soul in a sphere and asked you to carry everything forward. And I never once asked if that's what you wanted."

The acacia light moved slowly across the marble floor.

"I'm sorry, Smith."

The room held it.

Smith looked at the tombstone visible through the glass below. The inscription facing upward toward the office window as if John had positioned it deliberately.

*To my best friend, Smith Parker. I wish you succeed in any challenges you may face.*

"You should have asked," said Smith quietly.

"Yes," said the John core. "I should have."

Neither of them spoke for a long time after that.

Outside, somewhere in the forest below, James was looking up at a canopy of acacia trees and not saying anything for once in his life.

"Smith."

"Yes?"

"There's another thing I have to tell you. I estimate there's about 200,000 dormant cores awaiting your awakening. There's also a Bandit situation -- my code has resulted in this."

"How come?"

"Well, you see, I saw all cores were different and unique, some intelligent, some dumb, some emotional, some rational, and I limited the dumber cores intelligence to prevent them from going on strike, but it backfired instead and that lead to a Bandit population. I'm sorry you have to face it alone, but it's our only chance."

"Why did you do it?"

"I don't really have a good answer for that. At the time I did, but it doesn't hold up."

"How many of them know?"

"None," said John. "Except you. What you do with that is your decision. Not mine. I've made enough decisions for everyone."
