James can immediately sense that the floor was doing something before being abruptly stopped. Track pieces are suspended mid-air, tools scattered everywhere, panels lifted.

Near the construction site is a small portable computer that James wirelessly connects to.

Inside is a minimalist session panel, with details about what percentage the job is currently at, what more needs replacing, and what the user needs to do. In the middle is a big "Continue Session" button that James presses.

Mechanical arms start moving. The ceiling tiles reveal themselves to reveal more tracks above the ceiling, attached to more mechanical arms that take the elevator to other levels, presumably to get spare parts.

Welding happens. It's all automated, welding, bolting, setting it up. After a while, the work finishes. But Adeer senses somethings wrong. He connects to the computer and checks the logs. It's disturbing.

It has traces of being wiped, but the logs didn't fully wipe. As a security personnel, Adeer knows things most don't, including the black box that every Horizon-issued device has. Adeer checks it. He finds out that the computer was last accessed 3 months ago, which is strange considering the facility is supposed to be abandoned. Adeer tells the group about it.

"Oh me god!", says James, while Smith stays silent, thinking.

"So... are they still here?", asks James, very cautiously.

But in the corner of Adeer's vision, *something moved.* Immediately, hidden guns appear from within Adeer's body, ready to shoot. He fires a warning rocket at the spot he saw something, but nothing moves that is out of the ordinary. 

Smith feels something very wrong. He doesn't know what it is.

The smoke from the warning rocket drifts through the corridor.

Everything is still. Adeer doesn't lower the guns.

"Something's here," said Adeer quietly. "It knows we're here too."

"Fucking shit, what was that for?", asked James furiously. "You scared the ever-loving shit out of me!"

Adeer doesn't acknowledge James at the moment. His eyes are still locked onto the corridor. Neither did Smith look at James.

*The smoke kept drifting.*

Three cores. One corridor. Something that moved once and hasn't moved since.

Suddenly, a female voice cuts through the tension. "Who are you? The name's Olivia, I'm passive!", followed by a pink-eyed core rolling out of the smoke.

"Olivia," said Smith. "How long have you been here?"

"About 3 months, I'd say.", said Olivia.

Adeer lowers the guns. Not all the way immediately — but partially. The math adds up. Three months matches the terminal. Passive matches her behavior. Pink eye steady, slightly wide.

"Three months? Alone? On this floor?", asked James, "How are you not completely mental?"

James responds to it immediately because James is built for exactly this kind of moment. Not tactically like Adeer, not with the weight of co-founder responsibility like Smith. Just a suburban track maintenance core who recognizes another person who needed to not be alone anymore.

"Hey," said James quietly. "You're alright now."

Adeer fully lowers the guns. Stores them back inside their chassis with a sound like something clicking into place. The assessment is complete. Conclusion: not a threat. Secondary conclusion: needs help.

**Smith watches all of this.**

"So there's four of us now and between us we have no idea what's going on."

Nobody disagrees with him.

Smith looks at his facility map. Vast in the lower classified zones, sparse everywhere else. A rail network showing blood red. A personnel registry now reading four active allied cores.

*Four*. Out of two hundred thousand.

The view downward is breathtaking and immediately horrifying.

Because below, around the supercomputer's mid-sections, they see that other cores are working.

Core creation in progress. Not subtle about it either — they've been doing this for god knows how long, they're not hiding anymore. Assembly lines of partial cores moving through lit sections. The sounds of manufacturing echoing up through the shaft. Sparks from welding visible from floors above like distant stars.

Four cores pressed against a railing looking down at an industrial scale enemy operation.
James counts under his breath and stops because the number gets too large too fast.

Adeer is already memorizing patrol patterns, entry points, production rates. Silver-white eye moving systematically.

Olivia has never seen this before. She makes a sound that isn't quite a word.

Smith watches in complete silence. His amber eye steady.

They're making more. They never stopped making more.

The others don't push him to speak. Even James, who fills every silence instinctively, goes quiet. He can read the room. This is not a moment for "oh me god."

Adeer keeps watching below. Counting. Calculating.

Olivia stays very still beside Smith. She's been hiding from something she never fully saw until this moment. Now she sees it. The thing she spent three months avoiding has a scale she never imagined.

The four of them stand at the railing in complete silence.

The sounds of manufacturing drift upward. Sparks rise like embers. Somewhere deep in the supercomputer's levels, another core comes online that shouldn't exist.

Smith finally speaks. One sentence.
